# Laws from www.mom.gov.sg
**Date:** 2025-08-22

## About the Employment Act
**Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/employment-act

### AI Summary
The key changes to the Employment Act effective 1 April 2019 include expanded coverage to all employees (excluding seafarers, domestic workers, and certain public officials), redefined definitions of managers and executives based on job duties rather than salary, and new requirements for employers to provide key employment terms and itemized payslips to all covered workers.

## Responsible re-employment
**Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/re-employment

### AI Summary
The revised guidelines introduce several key changes: employers are now encouraged to adopt part-time re-employment with pro-rated EAP amounts, raise internal retirement and re-employment ages above statutory requirements, implement structured career planning for employees aged 45 and above, use job redesign to create age-friendly workplaces, and restructure medical benefits to include additional MediSave contributions or flexible benefits for healthcare coverage.

## Retirement
**Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/retirement

### AI Summary
The document updates the minimum retirement age to 63, effective from 1 August 2022, and introduces a Senior Employment Credit offering employers up to a 7% wage offset for hiring workers aged 60 and above, while also specifying a one-month window for appealing wrongful dismissals based on age to the Minister for Manpower.

## Work injury compensation
**Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/workplace-safety-and-health/work-injury-compensation

### AI Summary
The 2020 changes to the Work Injury Compensation Act (WICA) primarily focus on expanding coverage and increasing compensation limits. Key updates include extending protection to more non-manual workers, raising the salary cap for coverage, and enhancing compensation amounts for medical expenses, medical leave wages, and lump-sum payments for permanent incapacity or death. Additionally, the amendments streamline claim processes and clarify employer obligations regarding insurance and reporting to the Ministry of Manpower.

## About the Employment Act
**Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/employment-act

### AI Summary
The key changes to the Employment Act effective from 1 April 2019 include expanded coverage to all employees (with specific provisions for managers and executives), enhanced protections for workmen, and new requirements for employers to provide detailed key employment terms and itemized payslips. These amendments strengthen core employment rights and introduce clearer documentation standards for all covered workers.
